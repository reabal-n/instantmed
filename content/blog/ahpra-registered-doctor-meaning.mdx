When choosing an online doctor or healthcare service, you'll often see "AHPRA-registered" mentioned. But what does it actually mean, and why should you care?

## What Is AHPRA?

AHPRA stands for the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency. It's the national body responsible for registering and regulating health practitioners across Australia.

AHPRA works with 15 National Boards that set standards for different health professions, including doctors, nurses, pharmacists, psychologists, and more.

## What AHPRA Registration Means

When a doctor is AHPRA-registered, it means they have:

- Completed an approved medical degree
- Met the Medical Board of Australia's requirements
- Passed required examinations and assessments
- Met English language requirements
- No conditions preventing them from practising safely
- Agreed to follow professional standards and codes of conduct

<Callout variant="info">

Registration isn't a one-time thing. Doctors must renew annually and meet ongoing requirements including continuing professional development.

</Callout>

## Why It Matters for Telehealth

When using [online health services](/blog/what-is-telehealth "What is telehealth?"), AHPRA registration is your assurance that:

- The doctor is legally qualified to practise in Australia
- They're held to the same standards as any other doctor
- There's oversight and accountability
- You can verify their credentials yourself
- There's a complaints process if something goes wrong

## How to Verify a Doctor's Registration

You can check any health practitioner's registration on the AHPRA public register:

- Visit ahpra.gov.au and click "Check a practitioner"
- Search by name and profession
- View their registration status, type, and any conditions
- See which specialty (if any) they're registered in

<Callout variant="tip">

Legitimate telehealth services will openly display their doctors' names and registration details. If a service won't tell you who your doctor is, that's a red flag.

</Callout>

## Types of Medical Registration

- General registration - standard registration for practising doctors
- Specialist registration - for doctors with specialist qualifications
- Provisional registration - for doctors completing supervised training
- Limited registration - for specific purposes with conditions

## What If There's a Problem?

AHPRA handles complaints about registered health practitioners. If you have concerns about a doctor's conduct or care, you can:

- Lodge a complaint through AHPRA's website
- Contact the Health Care Complaints Commission in your state
- Raise concerns with the service provider first
- Seek a second opinion if you're unsure about advice received

## Beyond AHPRA: Other Quality Indicators

While AHPRA registration is essential, other factors indicate a quality telehealth service:

- Clear privacy policies and data protection
- Transparent pricing
- Responsive customer support
- Clear information about their doctors
- Appropriate clinical governance
- Willingness to refer when telehealth isn't suitable
