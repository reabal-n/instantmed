When choosing a healthcare service - particularly an online one - you will regularly encounter the phrase "AHPRA-registered." It appears on telehealth platforms, in medical practice websites, and in pharmacy credentials. Understanding what it actually means, what it requires of the practitioner, and how to verify it gives you the tools to evaluate any healthcare service with confidence.

## What AHPRA Is

AHPRA is the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency, established under the Health Practitioner Regulation National Law, which is enacted in all states and territories. It is the national body responsible for registering and regulating health practitioners across Australia.

AHPRA works alongside 15 National Boards, each governing a specific health profession. The Medical Board of Australia sets standards for medical practitioners, including requirements for registration, professional conduct, continuing professional development, and health and performance management.

As of the 2023-24 AHPRA Annual Report, the agency registers approximately 862,000 practitioners across 16 regulated health professions, including approximately 120,000 medical practitioners. This scale makes Australia's practitioner regulation one of the most comprehensive national systems in the world.

The 16 regulated professions under AHPRA include: medicine, nursing, midwifery, pharmacy, psychology, physiotherapy, optometry, dental practitioners, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health practitioners, Chinese medicine practitioners, chiropractors, medical radiation practitioners, occupational therapists, osteopaths, podiatrists, and paramedicine.

## What Registration Requires

AHPRA registration for a medical practitioner (doctor) requires meeting the Medical Board of Australia's standards. To obtain and maintain registration, a doctor must demonstrate:

**Qualifications:** Completion of an approved medical degree and, for specialist registration, completion of an approved specialist training program.

**Clinical training:** Completion of supervised clinical training programs after graduation (internship and residency).

**English language competency:** Meeting the Medical Board's English language standard, which requires demonstrated proficiency for international graduates.

**Criminal history:** Disclosure of any criminal history, which the Medical Board considers in assessing suitability to practise.

**Health:** Practitioners must not have any health conditions that would prevent safe practice. Practitioners must also have appropriate insurance, including professional indemnity insurance.

**Professional conduct:** Agreement to comply with the Medical Board's Code of Conduct, published as "Good Medical Practice: A Code of Conduct for Doctors in Australia." This document sets out the professional standards doctors are expected to meet, including standards for telehealth consultations.

<Callout variant="info">

The Medical Board of Australia's "Good Medical Practice" code applies to all consultations regardless of medium. A doctor conducting a telehealth consultation is subject to identical professional obligations as a doctor seeing a patient in person - including the obligation to take a thorough history, form a clinical opinion, and act in the patient's best interests.

</Callout>

## Types of Medical Registration

AHPRA issues several categories of medical registration:

**General registration:** The standard registration type for fully qualified medical practitioners who have completed all training requirements and met all Board standards. Most practising GPs and specialists hold general registration.

**Specialist registration:** Issued to doctors who have completed an approved specialist training program recognised by the Medical Board. Requires annual renewal alongside continuing professional development in the specialty.

**Provisional registration:** For doctors completing supervised clinical training in Australia, such as graduates undertaking their intern year. Provisional registrants must work under supervision and cannot practise independently.

**Limited registration:** Granted for specific purposes - for example, to allow an internationally trained doctor to work in an underserviced area, or to allow a teaching doctor to demonstrate procedures. Limited registration comes with conditions specifying its scope.

**Non-practising registration:** For practitioners who wish to maintain registration but are not currently practising (retired, on extended leave). Does not permit clinical practice.

For telehealth services, you are looking for a doctor with general or specialist registration - the registrations that permit independent clinical practice.

## How to Verify Any Practitioner

AHPRA maintains a public register available at ahpra.gov.au that anyone can search without an account or fee. The register is updated in real time.

**Step-by-step verification:**

1. Go to ahpra.gov.au
2. Click "Check registration" in the top navigation
3. Under "Find a practitioner," select the profession (Medical Practitioners)
4. Enter the practitioner's surname and/or first name
5. Select the matching result from the list
6. The full registration record shows: registration status, registration type, any conditions on registration, registration renewal date, and specialist registration (if any)

The result clearly shows whether registration is "Current," "Lapsed," "Suspended," or "Cancelled." Conditions on registration are also publicly visible - for example, if a doctor is required to practise under supervision.

<Callout variant="tip">

Legitimate telehealth services will openly display their practitioners' names and AHPRA registration numbers. If a service will not identify who reviewed your case, or refuses to provide a practitioner name on any documents issued, treat that as a significant red flag.

</Callout>

## Annual Renewal and Continuing Requirements

Registration is not permanent. Medical practitioners must renew their AHPRA registration annually, typically in September. The renewal process requires:

**Continuing Professional Development (CPD):** The Medical Board requires a minimum number of CPD hours annually - at least 50 hours per year, including specific categories of educational activity, peer review, and performance evaluation. This requirement is verified at renewal.

**Professional indemnity insurance:** All practising medical practitioners must hold current, adequate professional indemnity insurance. This protects patients in the event of negligent care.

**Declaration of health and conduct:** Annual declaration that no new conditions, criminal history, or other issues affect the practitioner's suitability to practise.

Registration that has lapsed or has not been renewed is immediately visible on the public register. A practitioner whose registration has lapsed cannot legally issue [medical certificates](/medical-certificate "Get a medical certificate"), [prescriptions](/prescriptions "Request a prescription"), or any clinical documentation.

## AHPRA's Notifications and Complaints Process

If you have concerns about a registered practitioner's conduct or the care you received, you can make a formal notification to AHPRA through its website. In 2022-23, AHPRA received approximately 9,000 notifications across all professions, with approximately 3,800 notifications about medical practitioners.

Notifications may result in investigation, conditions being placed on registration, or in serious cases, suspension or cancellation of registration. AHPRA can also refer practitioners to health tribunals for the most serious matters.

For telehealth consultations, the same standards and complaints processes apply as for in-person care. If you believe a telehealth consultation fell below the standard expected of a registered practitioner, you can lodge a notification with AHPRA.

## Why This Matters for Telehealth Specifically

Online healthcare services in Australia operate in a regulated environment. Any platform that allows a person to submit a medical request and receive clinical documentation - a certificate, a prescription, clinical advice - must use AHPRA-registered practitioners for that clinical function.

There is no "telehealth-only" registration category. A doctor conducting telehealth is subject to the same obligations as any other registered practitioner: taking an adequate history, exercising clinical judgment, acting in the patient's best interests, and maintaining appropriate records.

Platforms that advertise "instant" certificates or prescriptions with no clinical review, or that use AI to automatically approve requests without human medical review, are not providing the regulated healthcare service that AHPRA registration implies. A legitimate telehealth service involves a genuine clinical decision made by a registered doctor for each individual patient.

## Checking Other Health Practitioners

Beyond medical practitioners, AHPRA's register covers all 16 regulated health professions. This means you can verify:

- Nurse practitioners (registered nurses with extended prescribing authorities)
- Psychologists (relevant for telehealth [mental health](/conditions/depression "Depression and mental health support") services)
- Pharmacists (relevant for services that include pharmacist consultations)
- Physiotherapists, dentists, optometrists, and others who may offer telehealth services in their specialty

The same verification process applies across all professions: go to ahpra.gov.au, search by name, check the result.
