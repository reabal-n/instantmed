Health issues don't keep business hours. Whether it's late at night, early morning, or over the weekend, [online doctor services](/blog/what-is-telehealth "What is telehealth?") provide a convenient option when your regular GP isn't available.

## What After-Hours Telehealth Can Help With

After-hours online doctors can assist with many non-emergency conditions:

- Cold and flu symptoms
- Urinary tract infections
- Skin rashes and irritations
- Minor injuries and pain
- Mental health support
- Medication advice and refills
- Medical certificates for the next day
- General health questions

<Callout variant="emergency">

For emergencies - chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, or stroke symptoms - call 000 immediately. Telehealth is not for emergencies.

</Callout>

## After-Hours Service Hours

Different services operate different hours. Common patterns include:

- 24/7 services - available anytime, including public holidays
- Extended hours - typically 7am-10pm or similar
- After-hours only - evenings and weekends when GPs are closed
- On-demand services - you request when you need

## How After-Hours Telehealth Works

- You submit your request via app or website
- Provide your symptoms and relevant details
- A doctor reviews your information
- You consult via video, phone, or messaging
- Receive advice, prescriptions, or certificates as needed

Wait times vary by service and time. Late-night slots may have longer waits than evening or weekend appointments.

## Getting Prescriptions After Hours

After-hours doctors can prescribe most medications via [eScript](/blog/how-escripts-work "How eScripts work"). Keep in mind:

- You'll need a 24-hour pharmacy to fill it immediately
- Some medications have prescribing restrictions
- Controlled substances may not be available via telehealth
- The eScript is sent to your phone via SMS

<Callout variant="tip">

Search "24-hour pharmacy" plus your suburb to find late-night pharmacies near you. Major cities have several options.

</Callout>

## Medical Certificates After Hours

You can get a medical certificate after hours for:

- Illness that started in the evening or overnight
- Weekend illness when you need a certificate for Monday
- Conditions that worsened outside business hours
- When you simply couldn't see a doctor during the day

## Costs of After-Hours Services

After-hours telehealth may cost more than daytime services. Pricing varies by provider:

- Some charge the same rate 24/7
- Others have after-hours premiums
- Bulk-billing may not be available after hours
- Private services have set fees regardless of time

## Choosing Between Options

### Online Doctor

Best for: non-urgent issues, prescriptions, certificates, general advice when you can wait for a response.

### After-Hours GP Clinic

Best for: conditions needing physical examination, when you prefer face-to-face care.

### Hospital Emergency

Best for: genuine emergencies only. Not appropriate for minor illness or certificates.

### Healthdirect Helpline

Call 1800 022 222 for free health advice 24/7. Nurses can help you decide what level of care you need.
