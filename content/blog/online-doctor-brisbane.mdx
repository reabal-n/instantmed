Brisbane is Australia's third-largest city, home to over 2.5 million residents in the Greater Brisbane region according to the ABS 2021 Census, and growing faster than any other major Australian capital. The City of Brisbane itself recorded approximately 1.2 million residents at the 2021 Census, but the broader South East Queensland region -- stretching from the Sunshine Coast in the north to the Gold Coast in the south and Ipswich in the west -- represents a functional healthcare catchment of more than 3.8 million people.

The Brisbane North Primary Health Network and Brisbane South PHN have both flagged GP workforce distribution as a priority. Queensland's bulk-billing rate has come under increasing pressure as practice costs rise; while Queensland's statewide rate historically tracked near the national average of approximately 78% (Services Australia), inner Brisbane practices have shifted increasingly toward private billing, while outer growth corridors face genuine GP shortages relative to population.

Telehealth provides consistent, postcode-neutral access for non-emergency healthcare needs.

## What Telehealth Covers

An AHPRA-registered telehealth doctor can provide for Brisbane residents and workers:

- **Medical certificates**: Under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth) section 107, a certificate from any AHPRA-registered doctor satisfies the legal evidence standard for personal leave. Queensland employers -- whether private sector, state government, or Commonwealth agencies operating in Brisbane -- cannot refuse a valid telehealth certificate.
- **Repeat prescriptions**: For established medications you have been taking for 3+ months. Delivered as eScripts (SMS QR codes) fillable at any Queensland pharmacy.
- **General consultations**: Non-urgent conditions including respiratory symptoms, [skin concerns](/conditions/skin-rash "Skin rash and conditions") (with photos), urinary tract infections, [mental health support](/conditions/depression "Depression and mental health"), and medication management.
- **Referrals and pathology requests**: Issued digitally and taken to any specialist or collection centre (Queensland Medical Laboratory, Sullivan Nicolaides Pathology, and Dorevitch all operate across Brisbane).

## Brisbane by Region

Brisbane's geography -- with the river dividing the city and outer corridors expanding rapidly -- creates uneven access to primary care.

**Brisbane CBD and Inner City** (Brisbane City, South Brisbane, Fortitude Valley, Spring Hill, Petrie Terrace): Good GP density but high demand. Same-day bulk-billing appointments are rare in the inner city. Many practices operate on a mixed-billing model.

**Inner Northside** (Windsor, Lutwyche, Kedron, Stafford, Chermside): Reasonable coverage in established suburbs. Chermside has higher GP density around the Westfield precinct, but outer northside areas thin quickly.

**Inner Southside** (Woolloongabba, Annerley, Greenslopes, Mt Gravatt, Carindale): Moderate GP coverage. Greenslopes Private Hospital is a significant private health hub for this corridor.

**Eastern suburbs** (Wynnum, Manly, Cleveland, Capalaba): The Bayside corridor has reasonable GP coverage in established centres, but is geographically isolated from inner Brisbane without a car.

**Western suburbs** (Indooroopilly, Kenmore, Fig Tree Pocket, Jindalee, Brookfield): Mixed coverage. Established western suburbs have reasonable GP access; outer areas near the Ipswich border are more limited.

**Moreton Bay Region** (Redcliffe, Mango Hill, Narangba, North Lakes, Caboolture): One of Queensland's fastest-growing regions. The Moreton Bay Regional Council area experienced significant population growth in the 2016-2021 period, and healthcare infrastructure -- particularly general practice -- has not kept pace. GP wait times for new patient appointments in North Lakes and Mango Hill regularly extend to several weeks.

**Logan** (Logan Central, Beenleigh, Browns Plains, Springwood): Logan City is one of Australia's most diverse and rapidly growing municipalities. GP access per capita is below the Brisbane average, and Logan's demographics include a high proportion of families and young people who rely heavily on primary care.

**Ipswich** (Ipswich City, Springfield Lakes, Ripley, Redbank Plains): The Springfield Lakes and Ripley Valley corridors have seen extraordinary population growth. Springfield is one of Australia's largest master-planned communities, and the healthcare workforce there has expanded but remains stretched by demand.

## Queensland-Specific Healthcare Context

**DORA (Drug and Oral Reporting and Alerting System)**: Queensland operates DORA, the state's real-time prescription monitoring system for Schedule 8 (controlled) medications. DORA monitors prescribing and dispensing of opioids, benzodiazepines, and other Schedule 8 drugs across Queensland. Schedule 8 medications cannot be prescribed via asynchronous telehealth -- they require in-person assessment. DORA does not affect prescribing of the vast majority of medications used in everyday primary care.

**Queensland public sector employment**: Queensland public servants employed under the Public Service Act 2008 (Qld) and relevant enterprise agreements are required to provide medical certificates from registered health practitioners for absences. Certificates from AHPRA-registered doctors -- including telehealth certificates -- meet this requirement. Queensland Health, Education Queensland, and other Queensland Government agencies all accept telehealth-issued certificates.

**Queensland workers' compensation**: WorkCover Queensland manages workers' compensation claims in Queensland. For work-related illness or injury, standard telehealth certificates can initiate a claim, but WorkCover Queensland may require completion of their specific Work Capacity Certificate form for ongoing claims management.

## University Students in Brisbane

Brisbane has one of the highest concentrations of university students in Australia. Major institutions include the University of Queensland (UQ, St Lucia campus), Queensland University of Technology (QUT, Gardens Point and Kelvin Grove campuses), Griffith University (Nathan, Mt Gravatt, and Gold Coast campuses), James Cook University (Brisbane campus), Australian Catholic University (Brisbane campus), and several other institutions collectively enrolling over 150,000 students.

Most Brisbane universities accept medical certificates from AHPRA-registered doctors for academic consideration, exam-related application, and academic adjustment applications. Key points:

- UQ and QUT both accept telehealth-issued certificates for academic consideration applications
- The certificate should be dated contemporaneously with the affected assessment -- not obtained days or weeks after the fact
- Check whether your faculty requires the university's own form or whether a standard medical certificate is sufficient; requirements vary between faculties at the same institution
- All major Brisbane universities have student health services (UQ Health Care, QUT Medical Centre, Griffith Health Centre) -- these are often the fastest in-person option during exam periods when you need both documentation and clinical support
- International students with OSHC should check their policy terms -- telehealth private fees are typically not covered, but services are still accessible at private rates

## After-Hours Access

Brisbane's after-hours primary care options include:

- **National Home Doctor Service**: Operates across Greater Brisbane after hours
- **Brisbane City and inner-suburb after-hours clinics**: Several clinics operate until 10pm-midnight in major suburbs
- **Queensland Health's 13 HEALTH (13 43 25 84)**: A 24/7 phone health advice line for Queenslanders -- staffed by registered nurses who can advise on whether a presentation requires in-person care

**Major Brisbane emergency hospitals:**
- Royal Brisbane and Women's Hospital (RBWH), Herston: Queensland's largest hospital and the state's major trauma centre
- Princess Alexandra Hospital, Woolloongabba: Major tertiary hospital serving inner south Brisbane and Logan
- Mater Hospital, South Brisbane: Significant private and public hospital complex at South Brisbane
- Queen Elizabeth II Jubilee Hospital (QEII), Coopers Plains: Services the southside
- Logan Hospital, Meadowbrook: Major public hospital for the Logan and Beenleigh corridor
- The Prince Charles Hospital, Chermside: Cardiac centre for the northside
- **Call 000** for any life-threatening emergency

Telehealth operates 8am to 10pm AEST daily -- useful for evening needs where the alternative would be an after-hours clinic or emergency department.

## The Heat and Distance Factor

Brisbane's subtropical climate is a genuine healthcare access consideration. For someone who is unwell in summer (November to March), the prospect of driving 20-40 minutes to a GP in heat and humidity, sitting in a waiting room, and returning -- for a medical certificate, a repeat script, or an acute question -- is a real barrier to care.

Brisbane also has among the worst traffic in Australia for its size. The 2023 INRIX Global Traffic Scorecard ranked Brisbane among the most congested cities in Australia. Outer suburb residents face 30-60 minute commutes to inner-city GP practices that may not have same-day availability.

Telehealth eliminates both barriers. A consultation can be completed from home in air-conditioned comfort, with the outcome delivered digitally within hours.

## When In-Person Care Is Necessary

Telehealth is not appropriate for conditions requiring physical examination:

- [Ear infections](/conditions/ear-infection "Ear infection treatment"), abdominal assessment, chest auscultation -- these require physical presence
- Injuries requiring imaging or wound management
- Any presentation where clinical examination is necessary for a safe decision

If a telehealth doctor determines in-person assessment is needed, they will advise the appropriate care pathway. That is the system working correctly.

## Pharmacy Access and eScripts

Brisbane has excellent pharmacy coverage across all suburbs and shopping centres. All pharmacies in Queensland participate in the national Prescription Exchange Service and accept eScripts. Present the QR code SMS to the pharmacist -- no additional steps required. Major chains (Chemist Warehouse, Priceline, Terry White, Discount Drug Stores, Good Price Pharmacy Warehouse) and thousands of independent pharmacies are located throughout Greater Brisbane.

PBS co-payments: general patients pay up to $31.60 per item; concession card holders pay up to $7.70 (Services Australia, 2025-26). PBS pricing is the same regardless of how the prescription was obtained.

## How the Service Works

1. Complete the online intake form (5-10 minutes) describing your symptoms and what you need.
2. An AHPRA-registered doctor reviews your case -- a genuine clinical assessment, not an automated process.
3. If clinically appropriate, the outcome is delivered digitally: PDF certificate by email, or eScript by SMS.
4. If in-person assessment is needed, the doctor advises the appropriate level of care.

Review times are typically 30-90 minutes during business hours, 1-4 hours in the evening. The service operates 8am to 10pm AEST, 7 days a week.
