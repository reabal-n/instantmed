Adelaide is the capital of South Australia and home to approximately 1.4 million residents in the Greater Adelaide statistical area according to the ABS 2021 Census. Adelaide is often described as compact relative to other Australian capitals, but this characterisation understates the genuine access gaps that exist across the metropolitan footprint -- particularly in the northern suburbs corridor extending toward Salisbury and Elizabeth, and the southern corridor reaching toward Noarlunga, Morphett Vale, and the Onkaparinga council area.

The Adelaide Primary Health Network has identified GP workforce distribution and after-hours access as persistent service gaps, particularly in the outer northern and southern suburbs where population growth has outpaced the development of primary care infrastructure. South Australia's bulk-billing rate has been below the national average of approximately 78% (Services Australia) in several metropolitan areas, with inner-city and mid-eastern suburbs shifting largely to private billing over the past decade.

Telehealth provides consistent, postcode-neutral access for non-emergency healthcare needs.

## What Telehealth Covers

An AHPRA-registered telehealth doctor can provide for Adelaide residents and workers:

- **Medical certificates**: Under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth) section 107, a certificate from any AHPRA-registered doctor satisfies the legal evidence standard for personal leave. SA employers -- whether private sector, state government, or Commonwealth agencies operating in Adelaide -- cannot refuse a valid telehealth certificate.
- **Repeat prescriptions**: For established medications you have been taking for 3+ months. Delivered as eScripts (SMS QR codes) fillable at any South Australian pharmacy.
- **General consultations**: Non-urgent conditions including respiratory symptoms, skin concerns (with photos), urinary tract infections, mental health support, and medication management.
- **Referrals and pathology requests**: Issued digitally and taken to any specialist or collection centre (SA Pathology operates collection centres across metropolitan Adelaide).

## Adelaide by Region

Adelaide's grid layout and the defining ring of parklands make its geography more navigable than many Australian cities, but access to primary care still varies significantly by area.

**Adelaide CBD and North Adelaide**: Good GP coverage in and around the city centre. North Adelaide has a concentration of private practices serving the inner north. Bulk-billing is uncommon in these areas; private fees of $50-90 above the Medicare rebate are standard.

**Eastern Suburbs** (Norwood, Burnside, Kensington, Magill, Campbelltown): Well-serviced with a mix of private and mixed-billing practices. This is among Adelaide's most GP-accessible areas outside the immediate CBD.

**Inner Southern Suburbs** (Unley, Mitcham, Edwardstown, Glenelg): Good coverage. Several established GP practices around the Unley/Mitcham corridor, and extended-hours options along the beachside strip.

**Northern Corridor** (Prospect, Enfield, Salisbury, Elizabeth, Para Hills, Angle Vale): The inner northern suburbs (Prospect, Enfield) have reasonable GP access. Moving north through Salisbury, Elizabeth, and the outer growth areas, GP density thins considerably. Elizabeth, historically one of Adelaide's larger planned communities, has community health centre services but limited private GP options. Angle Vale and Blakeview are growth-area suburbs where new housing significantly outruns healthcare infrastructure.

**Southern Corridor** (Marion, Brighton, Morphett Vale, Noarlunga, Aldinga): Marion and Brighton have moderate GP access. The outer southern corridor through Morphett Vale to Noarlunga and Aldinga Beach has fewer bulk-billing options and longer wait times for new patient bookings.

**Western Suburbs** (Henley Beach, Grange, Semaphore, Port Adelaide): The western beachside suburbs have reasonable coverage, though mixed billing is increasingly common. Port Adelaide has community health services and SA Health facilities.

**Adelaide Hills** (Stirling, Aldgate, Hahndorf, Nairne, Mount Barker): The Hills represent Adelaide's sharpest access gap. Residents in Hahndorf, Nairne, and outer Hills townships face drives of 20-40 minutes to reach a GP in the metro area. The Mount Barker district has growing GP services but demand consistently exceeds supply.

## SA-Specific Healthcare Context

**SAPIS (South Australia Prescription Information Service)**: SA Health operates SAPIS, the state's real-time prescription monitoring system for Schedule 8 (controlled) medications -- opioids, benzodiazepines, and other controlled drugs. Schedule 8 medications cannot be prescribed via asynchronous telehealth. For the vast majority of medications used in everyday primary care, SAPIS has no effect on the prescribing process.

**SA public sector employment**: SA public servants employed under the Public Sector Act 2009 (SA) and relevant enterprise agreements are required to provide medical certificates from registered health practitioners for personal leave. Certificates from AHPRA-registered doctors -- including telehealth certificates -- meet this requirement. SA Health, TAFE SA, and other SA Government agency employees are all covered by the same standard.

**SA workers' compensation**: ReturnToWorkSA manages workers' compensation claims in South Australia. For work-related illness or injury, standard telehealth certificates can initiate a claim. ReturnToWorkSA may require completion of their specific WorkCover certificate form for ongoing claims management.

## University Students in Adelaide

Adelaide's higher education landscape changed significantly in 2024 with the merger of the University of Adelaide and the University of South Australia (UniSA) to form Adelaide University, one of the largest universities in Australia with approximately 90,000 students enrolled. Flinders University (Bedford Park campus, with medical facilities at Flinders Medical Centre) and Torrens University Australia also operate in Adelaide.

Key points for Adelaide university students:

- Adelaide University and Flinders University both accept medical certificates from AHPRA-registered doctors for special consideration and deferred examination applications
- The certificate should be dated contemporaneously with the affected assessment period -- documentation obtained weeks after the fact is less persuasive
- Check whether your faculty requires the university's own medical certificate form or whether a standard certificate is sufficient; requirements vary between faculties and between the merged Adelaide University's inherited campuses
- All Adelaide universities have student health services -- these are often the best option when you need both clinical support and documentation
- International students should check their OSHC policy terms; telehealth private fees are typically not covered but the service is accessible at private rates

## After-Hours Access

Adelaide's after-hours primary care options include:

- **National Home Doctor Service**: Operates across metropolitan Adelaide after hours
- **HouseCall Doctor**: Another after-hours home visit service operating in Adelaide
- **SA Health Direct (1800 022 222)**: A 24/7 health advice line staffed by registered nurses who can advise on care pathways

**Major Adelaide emergency hospitals:**
- Royal Adelaide Hospital (RAH), Adelaide CBD: SA's major tertiary hospital and state trauma centre, opened 2017 on North Terrace
- Flinders Medical Centre (FMC), Bedford Park: Major tertiary hospital serving the southern suburbs
- Women's and Children's Hospital (WCH), North Adelaide: SA's dedicated paediatric and women's hospital
- Lyell McEwin Hospital, Elizabeth Vale: Services the northern suburbs corridor
- Modbury Hospital, Modbury: Services the northeastern suburbs (operating as an SA Health facility)
- The Queen Elizabeth Hospital (TQEH), Woodville: Services the western suburbs
- Noarlunga Hospital, Noarlunga Downs: Services the outer southern suburbs
- **Call 000** for any life-threatening emergency

Telehealth operates 8am to 10pm AEST (7:30am-9:30pm Adelaide time in SA's standard time, ACST UTC+9:30) daily -- useful for Adelaide residents during evening hours when most after-hours clinic options have closed.

## Adelaide's Time Zone

South Australia operates on ACST (UTC+9:30), 30 minutes behind AEST (UTC+10). InstantMed's operating hours of 8am-10pm AEST correspond to 7:30am-9:30pm Adelaide time for most of the year. During South Australia's daylight saving period (approximately October to April, when SA advances to ACDT, UTC+10:30), Adelaide time is 30 minutes ahead of AEST, so service operates 8:30am-10:30pm Adelaide time.

## When In-Person Care Is Necessary

Telehealth is not appropriate for conditions requiring physical examination:

- Ear infections, abdominal assessment, chest auscultation -- these require physical presence
- Injuries requiring imaging or wound management
- Any presentation where clinical examination is necessary for a safe decision

If a telehealth doctor determines in-person assessment is needed, they will advise the appropriate care pathway. That is the system working correctly.

## Pharmacy Access and eScripts

Adelaide has good pharmacy coverage across all suburbs and shopping precincts. All pharmacies in South Australia participate in the national Prescription Exchange Service and accept eScripts. Present the QR code SMS to the pharmacist -- no additional steps required. Major chains (Chemist Warehouse, Priceline, Terry White, Amcal, Capital Chemist) and independent pharmacies are located throughout metropolitan Adelaide.

PBS co-payments: general patients pay up to $31.60 per item; concession card holders pay up to $7.70 (Services Australia, 2025-26). PBS pricing is the same regardless of how the prescription was obtained.

## How the Service Works

1. Complete the online intake form (5-10 minutes) describing your symptoms and what you need.
2. An AHPRA-registered doctor reviews your case -- a genuine clinical assessment, not an automated process.
3. If clinically appropriate, the outcome is delivered digitally: PDF certificate by email, or eScript by SMS.
4. If in-person assessment is needed, the doctor advises the appropriate level of care.

Review times are typically 30-90 minutes during business hours, 1-4 hours in the evening. The service operates 8am to 10pm AEST, 7 days a week.
