Illness during exam period or when a major assignment is due creates a layered problem: you are unwell, you are behind, and you need documentation quickly while managing both. Australian universities have processes for this - academic consideration, extensions, and exam-related applications - but the requirements are often stricter and more specific than what your employer needs for a sick day.

Here is a complete guide to what universities require, how to get the right documentation, and how to navigate the process without making things worse.

## The Three Types of Academic Accommodation

Understanding what you are applying for shapes what documentation you need.

### Assessment Timing Request

A short extension on a submission deadline - typically 1 to 7 days depending on university policy and the severity of your circumstances. Many universities have automated extension systems that require minimal documentation for short extensions (1-2 days) but need a certificate for anything longer.

### Academic Documentation

A formal process for circumstances that affected your performance in an assessment or your ability to complete it at all. Outcomes vary: adjusted marking, supplementary assessment, a grade of Aegrotat (passed on assessed work), or withdrawal without academic penalty. Academic consideration is the route for exams, major assignments, and in-class assessments.

### Exam-Related Application

Sitting the exam at a later date - usually the supplementary exam period. The process and documentation requirements are similar to academic consideration but specifically for examinations.

## What Your Certificate Must Say

University requirements are generally more specific than workplace requirements. A certificate for university purposes should include:

- Your full name (matching your student record)
- The date of the consultation
- A statement that you were unfit for study, examination, or academic activity (not just "unfit for work" - though this phrasing may be accepted at some institutions)
- The dates affected - the consultation must overlap with or closely follow the assessment period
- A statement on severity or impact - some universities require the doctor to comment on whether your capacity to study or perform was significantly affected
- The practitioner's name, AHPRA registration number, signature, and practice details

<Callout variant="tip">

Check your university's specific requirements before your consultation. Some universities have their own declaration forms that the doctor must complete. Others require specific wording. A certificate that does not match the university's requirements can delay or prevent your application from being approved.

</Callout>

## University-Specific Processes

### University of Sydney

Applications for Academic Documentation go through the online Student Administration portal. The deadline is typically 5 business days after the affected assessment. Medical certificates must be from a registered medical or health practitioner. The University of Sydney accepts telehealth certificates.

### University of Melbourne

The Student Equity and Disability Support office handles academic adjustments for ongoing conditions. For acute illness, applications go through Stop 1 (student services). The deadline is generally 2 working days after the affected assessment. Melbourne requires that the medical practitioner have a treating relationship with you for ongoing conditions - a one-off consultation certificate may not satisfy this requirement for extended situations.

### UNSW

UNSW uses an online Academic Documentation portal. Applications must be submitted within 3 days of the affected assessment. UNSW accepts certificates from registered health practitioners, including telehealth certificates.

### Monash University

Monash processes Academic Documentation through their student portal. The deadline is 3 business days after the affected assessment. Monash requires that the certificate cover the period of the assessment and state that your capacity to study or sit the exam was affected.

### QUT, Griffith, UTS, and others

Most Australian universities follow a similar pattern: online application, 2-5 business days after the assessment, certificate covering the relevant dates, and a requirement that the certificate comment on your capacity to study or perform. Your university's student services or faculty office can confirm the exact requirements.

<Callout variant="info">

Most university applications require the certificate to be submitted within 2-5 business days of the affected assessment. Do not wait until your results come out. Apply when you are affected - even if you attempt the exam and feel you performed poorly due to illness.

</Callout>

## The Timing Problem

The most common mistake students make is waiting too long. University academic consideration processes have strict deadlines - typically 2-5 business days after the affected date. Applying after results are released is almost universally rejected.

**The correct sequence is:**

1. Realise you are unwell and the illness is affecting an upcoming or current assessment
2. Consult a doctor on the same day or as soon as possible - telehealth means you can do this from your room
3. Obtain the certificate covering the affected dates
4. Submit your academic consideration application before the deadline
5. Attend the exam or submit what you can, if at all possible - attempting with illness is usually better than not attempting at all

A certificate obtained days or weeks after the event will raise questions about why you did not seek care at the time. Same-day documentation is strongest.

## Getting the Certificate During Exam Period

Exam period is one of the worst times to try to get a GP appointment - clinics are busy, and your mental and physical energy is limited. [Telehealth](/consult "Consult a doctor") is particularly useful here:

- You can consult from your student accommodation or home
- No travel time or waiting room
- Certificate is delivered digitally - you can upload it to your university's portal directly
- Available 7 days a week, including during holidays
- No need to leave your study environment when you need to be resting and recovering

When you consult via telehealth for a university certificate, mention that it is for university academic consideration rather than work. The doctor should know to comment on your study capacity rather than just your fitness for employment.

## Mental Health and Academic Documentation

Mental health conditions are valid grounds for academic consideration, but the documentation requirements are more nuanced than for physical illness.

**Acute mental health episodes** - a [panic attack](/conditions/panic-attacks "Panic attacks") on the day of an exam, a depressive episode affecting study in the week before an assessment - are handled similarly to acute physical illness. A certificate from a GP, psychologist, or other registered practitioner documenting the impact on your capacity is appropriate.

**Ongoing mental health conditions** - if you have a diagnosed anxiety disorder, [depression](/conditions/depression "Depression"), ADHD, or another condition that regularly affects your studies, the appropriate pathway is your university's disability or accessibility services rather than repeated academic consideration applications. Disability services can provide ongoing adjustments (extra time in exams, alternative assessment arrangements, extensions without case-by-case applications) that better suit chronic conditions.

Your university counselling service - available free to enrolled students - can provide supporting documentation for academic consideration applications and can also assist with the application process.

## University Accessibility and Disability Services

If your condition is ongoing rather than a one-off acute event, registering with your university's disability or accessibility service is the better long-term solution. Once registered:

- You receive ongoing adjustments without needing to reapply each assessment period
- Exam adjustments (extra time, rest breaks, separate rooms, readers or scribes) are available
- Assignment flexibility may be built into your accommodations
- You have a documented relationship with the university that makes future applications smoother

Conditions that can qualify include: physical disabilities, chronic health conditions, mental health disorders, neurodevelopmental conditions (ADHD, autism, dyslexia), vision or hearing impairments, and others. You will need supporting documentation from a relevant health professional when you register, but this is a one-time process rather than a per-assessment application.

## What Happens If Academic Documentation Is Approved

Outcomes vary by university, faculty, and assessment type. Common outcomes include:

- **Assessment extension**: More time to submit
- **Supplementary examination**: Sitting a separate exam, usually in the examination period at the end of semester or early next semester
- **Deferred result**: Marked assessment or result held pending a supplementary opportunity
- **Adjusted marking**: Results weighted toward other components of the course
- **Withdrawal without academic penalty**: Removal from the course without a fail grade on your transcript (most universities have late withdrawal options for medical circumstances)
- **Aegrotat pass**: A pass granted based on prior work without the need to sit the assessment

## What Happens If Your Application Is Denied

If your application is denied, you have appeal rights. The process varies by university but generally involves:

1. Formal review by a different assessor (academic appeals panel or student ombudsman)
2. Providing additional documentation to support your case
3. Formal complaint if the review is also unsuccessful

Your student union or student guild has free advocacy services that assist with academic consideration appeals. These advocates know the process, understand the grounds for appeal, and can write or review your appeal submission. This is an underused resource - most students do not know it exists.

<Callout variant="tip">

If you are a first-year student navigating this for the first time, your student union advocacy service can walk you through the entire process. They have helped many students in exactly your situation and can tell you what documentation strengthens an application and what common errors to avoid.

</Callout>

## A Note on Honesty

Universities see a high volume of academic consideration applications, particularly around exam periods. Applications that are clearly genuine and well-documented are approved. Applications that have inconsistencies, weak documentation, or implausible timing raise questions.

The best approach is to see a doctor when you are genuinely unwell - not to work backwards from a desired outcome. A telehealth certificate obtained the same day you are affected is straightforward and credible. A certificate obtained after results are released is not.
