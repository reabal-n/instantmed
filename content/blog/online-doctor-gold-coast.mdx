The Gold Coast is Australia's sixth-largest city, home to over 620,000 residents according to the Australian Bureau of Statistics 2021 Census, and host to roughly 13 million visitors each year according to Tourism and Events Queensland. That combination of permanent population, domestic tourism, and international visitors puts real pressure on local primary care. Finding a bulk-billing GP appointment at short notice on the Gold Coast is genuinely difficult, and getting more difficult as the city grows.

The Gold Coast Primary Health Network has flagged GP workforce shortages as a persistent structural challenge. The region's bulk-billing rate consistently sits below the national average, and new patient wait times at many practices extend to several weeks. For residents without an established GP - or for the many visitors who fall ill on holiday - the alternatives are an after-hours clinic with an unpredictable wait, or the emergency department.

Telehealth provides a third option for the majority of non-emergency healthcare needs.

## What Telehealth Covers on the Gold Coast

An AHPRA-registered doctor can assess and manage a wide range of conditions without requiring you to be present in a clinic:

- **Medical certificates**: For work absence, study leave, or special consideration applications at Bond or Griffith University. Under the Fair Work Act 2009, a certificate from any AHPRA-registered doctor - including a telehealth doctor - meets the legal evidence standard for personal leave.
- **Repeat prescriptions**: For established medications you have been taking for 3+ months. Prescriptions are delivered as eScripts (QR code by SMS) and can be filled at any pharmacy on the Gold Coast.
- **General consultations**: For non-urgent health concerns where physical examination is not required - respiratory symptoms, skin conditions (with photo), urinary tract infections, mental health support.
- **Referrals and pathology requests**: A telehealth doctor can issue a specialist referral or pathology request that you take to a collection centre.

## Suburb Coverage: The Whole Corridor

Telehealth works anywhere with an internet connection, which matters on the Gold Coast where healthcare services cluster around Southport and Robina, leaving residents in other areas underserved.

**Northern Gold Coast**: Coomera, Helensvale, Ormeau, Pimpama - these are the fast-growing northern suburbs where new housing developments have outpaced the establishment of medical services. Telehealth is particularly valuable here.

**Central Gold Coast**: Southport, Labrador, Bundall, Surfers Paradise, Broadbeach, Mermaid Beach - better GP coverage but high demand and limited bulk-billing.

**Southern corridor**: Burleigh Heads, Palm Beach, Currumbin, Varsity Lakes, Robina, Coolangatta - the southern suburbs have some bulk-billing practices but waits are common.

**Hinterland**: Mudgeeraba, Nerang, Springbrook, Tamborine Mountain, Mount Tamborine - the most geographically isolated communities on the Gold Coast, where driving to a clinic can take 30-45 minutes. Telehealth removes this barrier entirely.

## University Students on the Gold Coast

The Gold Coast has two major universities: Bond University in Robina and Griffith University's Gold Coast campus in Southport. Together they have approximately 40,000 students enrolled, many from interstate or overseas who may not have an established GP locally.

Students commonly need:
- Medical certificates for special consideration applications when illness affects exam performance
- Repeat prescriptions for medications they normally obtain from a GP at home
- General health advice when they are unsure whether a symptom warrants a clinic visit

Both Bond and Griffith accept medical certificates from AHPRA-registered doctors for academic purposes, including telehealth-issued certificates. For special consideration applications, the certificate should be dated contemporaneously with the affected assessment - not obtained weeks later.

> **Tip:** If you are a student and too unwell to attend an assessment, initiate your special consideration application through your university's student portal that day, even before you have the certificate. This creates a contemporaneous record. Then complete the telehealth consultation to obtain the documentation.

## Visitors and Tourists

The Gold Coast receives millions of domestic and international visitors annually. Common reasons visitors seek medical care:
- Illness during a family holiday
- Gastroenteritis from a restaurant meal
- Skin conditions from sun exposure
- Medication running out or being left at home

For visitors who are unwell but not severely ill, telehealth offers a practical solution. You can request a certificate for your employer back home - a certificate from an AHPRA-registered doctor is valid for any Australian employer regardless of where you happen to be physically located.

International visitors in Australia can access telehealth services if they are 18+ and physically in Australia. The medical certificate will be valid for Australian employers. For international employer requirements, check what documentation your home country and employer require - these vary significantly.

> **Info:** If you need a prescription while visiting the Gold Coast, your eScript can be filled at any pharmacy in Australia. You are not limited to a specific pharmacy or chain.

## After-Hours and Seasonal Demand

The Gold Coast has predictable healthcare pressure points:

| Peak period | Healthcare impact |
|---|---|
| Christmas/New Year (Dec-Jan) | Highest tourist volume, clinics stretched, pharmacies in tourist areas extended hours |
| Schoolies (late November) | Young adult presentations increase, emergency services under pressure |
| Easter long weekend | Short-staffed clinics, higher after-hours demand |
| Major events (G20, Commonwealth Games, Gold Coast 500) | Event-related injuries and illness, visitor population surge |

After-hours GP access is a known gap on the Gold Coast. While there are several after-hours clinics, they cluster around Southport and Robina. Residents in Coolangatta, the hinterland, or northern growth corridors face a significant drive to the nearest open clinic after 6pm. Telehealth operates 8am to 10pm AEST daily and fills this gap for non-emergency needs.

## When In-Person Care Is Necessary

Telehealth is not appropriate for all presentations. Attend a clinic or emergency department in person for:

- Any symptoms requiring physical examination: ear infections, throat inspections, abdominal pain assessment
- Injuries requiring X-ray or wound assessment
- Presentations where clinical examination is needed to make a safe decision

**Gold Coast emergency resources:**
- Gold Coast University Hospital (GCUH), Parklands Boulevard, Southport: the Gold Coast's main tertiary hospital with a 24/7 emergency department
- Robina Hospital, Robina: 24/7 emergency department serving the southern suburbs
- **Call 000** for any life-threatening emergency - chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke signs (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty), severe injury

## Pharmacy Access and eScripts

The Gold Coast has excellent pharmacy coverage across all major suburbs and shopping precincts. Every Australian pharmacy accepts eScripts - the QR code on your phone. You do not need to use a specific pharmacy or one that "accepts" your telehealth service. Chemist Warehouse, Priceline, Terry White, Capital Chemist, and all independent pharmacies participate in the national Prescription Exchange Service.

Pharmacies in tourist areas (Surfers Paradise, Broadbeach, Coolangatta) tend to maintain extended trading hours during peak season.

## How Telehealth Works: The Process

1. Complete the online intake form - describe your symptoms, when they started, and what you need. This takes 5-10 minutes.
2. An AHPRA-registered doctor reviews your case. This is a genuine clinical assessment.
3. If the request is clinically appropriate, the outcome is delivered digitally: a PDF certificate emailed to you, or an eScript sent by SMS.
4. If the doctor needs more information, they send a follow-up question. Respond promptly to avoid delays.
5. If in-person assessment is needed, the doctor will say so and advise the appropriate level of care.

Under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth), section 107, a certificate from an AHPRA-registered doctor satisfies the evidence requirement for personal leave. The Fair Work Ombudsman has confirmed that telehealth-issued certificates meet this standard.

## Medicare and Costs

Asynchronous telehealth services (form-based, no real-time call) are private fee services and do not attract a Medicare rebate. The fee is typically $19.95-$49.95 depending on the service type.

If you want a Medicare-rebatable telehealth consultation, you need a real-time video or phone consultation with a GP practice that is registered for Medicare telehealth billing. Many Gold Coast GP practices offer this - contact your regular practice to check availability.

PBS pricing at the pharmacy is the same regardless of how your prescription was obtained. General patients pay up to $31.60 per PBS item; concession card holders pay up to $7.70 (Services Australia, 2025-26 rates).
