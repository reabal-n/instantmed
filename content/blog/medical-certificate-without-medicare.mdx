Whether you're an international student, a tourist, here on a work visa, or simply don't have Medicare, you can still access medical certificates in Australia. Here's how.

## Who Doesn't Have Medicare?

- International students (most visa types)
- Tourists and short-term visitors
- Some working visa holders
- New migrants waiting for Medicare processing
- Visitors from countries without reciprocal healthcare agreements

## Your Options Without Medicare

### Private Telehealth Services

Many [telehealth services](/blog/what-is-telehealth "What is telehealth?") don't require Medicare. You pay a flat fee for the consultation, the same as any other patient.

- Usually same price for everyone regardless of Medicare status
- Quick and convenient
- No need to find a clinic that accepts non-Medicare patients
- Certificates delivered digitally

### Private GP Clinics

You can visit any GP clinic - you'll just pay privately. Expect to pay around $70-$120 for a standard consultation.

### University Health Services

If you're a student, your university likely has a health service that caters to international students. Many offer reduced rates or are covered by your Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC).

## Using Your Health Insurance

### Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

International students must have OSHC. This covers:

- GP visits (you may pay and claim back)
- Some specialist consultations
- Hospital treatment
- Some prescription medications

<Callout variant="tip">

Check if your OSHC provider has preferred GPs or telehealth partners - you may pay less or get direct billing.

</Callout>

### Travel Insurance

If you're a visitor, your travel insurance may cover medical consultations. Check your policy for:

- Coverage for general illness (not just accidents)
- Whether telehealth is covered
- Claim process (pay upfront or direct billing)
- Any excess or deductibles

## Reciprocal Healthcare Agreements

Australia has healthcare agreements with some countries. Visitors from these countries may access Medicare:

- United Kingdom
- Ireland
- New Zealand
- Belgium, Finland, Italy, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Slovenia, Sweden

Coverage varies by country. Check the Services Australia website for details specific to your country.

## What to Expect Without Medicare

- You'll pay upfront for consultations
- Fees may be higher than bulk-billed services
- Prescription medications won't be PBS-subsidised
- You'll need to manage your own health records
- Follow-up care is your responsibility to arrange

## Tips for Accessing Care

- Keep your health insurance details handy
- Ask about fees upfront
- Request itemised receipts for insurance claims
- Use telehealth when appropriate - often simpler than finding clinics
- For ongoing conditions, establish care with a regular GP

<Callout variant="info">

Your medical certificate will be just as valid as anyone else's. Medicare status doesn't affect the legitimacy of the certificate.

</Callout>
