# A trustworthy contact page, undone by one stray line of copy

The page reads like a real clinic. Warm ivory, AHPRA signals where they should be, a doctor's signature in the footer. One misplaced billing line under the response gauge is doing real damage, and the form asks for more than it needs.

## What's working

- Hero feels like a clinic, not a startup. Type pairing (Plus Jakarta Sans with Source Sans 3) earns its keep.
- AHPRA trust markers are placed where a hesitant patient will actually see them.
- Footer signature, "What we won't do" link, and visible response metrics. The signature devices are the strongest part of the page.
- Mobile card rhythm is calm. Easy to scroll, easy to scan.

## What to fix, ranked

### 1. The "Refund if declined" line under the response gauge

**Impact:** high · **Where:** support response section, around the 0:12 mark

This reads as a copy-paste bug. Refunds belong next to a consult outcome, not a support SLA. A patient sees it and wonders if the rest of the page is also half-finished. Replace with something that fits the gauge ("Resolved on first contact" works), or remove it.

### 2. The contact form asks for both Category and Subject

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** support form, at 0:17

If the category is already selected, the subject line is just typing tax on a phone. Drop the Subject field. Category plus message body is enough to route an inquiry, and every field you remove lifts submission rate.

### 3. Active-state styling on the category buttons

**Impact:** low · **Where:** "General Inquiry" selector, at 0:16

The solid dark-blue fill lands too hard on the ivory canvas. A tinted background with a defined border holds the same job without shouting. Stripe-grade polish, small change.

## Full critique by category

**Brand spine (8).** Reads Australian, reads clinical, reads warm. "Faster than your GP" is missing from the hero, which is a free win sitting on the table.

**Color and surface (8).** The ivory does its job. Only the active button state breaks the calm.

**Motion (7).** The mobile nav tray slides in on a flat linear ease and feels mechanical (at 0:26). A spring curve like cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) costs nothing and lands properly.

**Copy voice (7).** Mostly unhurried, mostly right. The "Refund if declined" line is the headline problem. Separately, "no chatbot wall" appears twice in the same viewport at 0:07. Say it once, and let the body copy talk about how quickly a human actually replies.

**Hierarchy and layout (8).** The "Contact Us" pill at the top of the contact page is telling people where they already are. Remove it and let the hero breathe.

**Conversion friction (8).** The bottom-of-page conversion module is sharp. The support form is the only thing dragging the score.

## Reference frame

Mosh would keep the warmth and cut the Subject field on instinct. Pilot would push "Faster than your GP" into the hero and make the response gauge the trust anchor. Stripe would never ship the solid-blue active state, and Linear would have caught the misplaced refund line in review. The bones are at that level. The finish needs one more pass.
