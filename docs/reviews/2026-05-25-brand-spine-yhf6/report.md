# The thesis lands in three seconds. The compliance row lets it down.

The brand reads clearly from the first frame: price upfront, refund visible, regulators named below the fold. It already sits next to Pilot and Mosh on the shelf. A handful of layout and contrast fixes would push it to nine.

## What's working

- Hero states the offer plainly. No throat-clearing.
- CTAs carry the price and the refund line. Friction stays low.
- LegitScript live check and the response-time sticky bar do the trust work without shouting.
- Copy reads like a GP, not a marketer. Refund, terms, and timing all stated outright.
- Ivory background with deep-blue buttons. Calm, clinical, easy on the eyes.

## What to fix, ranked

### 1. Restyle the compliance logo row on the homepage

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** homepage hero, just under the fold (see frame at 0:04)

The compliance logos are doing important work but they're cramped and washed out in low-contrast grey. That's the row that has to convince a sceptical adult we're real. Give it horizontal breathing room and pick one monochrome treatment with enough weight to actually read on mobile.

### 2. Two-column the Related Health Topics on mobile

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** Conditions and Symptoms footer blocks (around the 2:08 mark)

Single-column link lists drag the page out for no reason. A two-column grid halves the scroll and makes the footer feel considered instead of dumped.

### 3. Fix the badge clipping on the certificate mock

**Impact:** low · **Where:** certificate preview section (around 0:32)

The green "Delivered to inbox" pill clips the edge of the mock document. Small thing, but it's the hero proof artefact. Lift the z-index or nudge the pill so it sits cleanly outside the card.

## Full critique by category

**Brand spine (9).** The promise is legible immediately and the regulatory backing is right where a cautious buyer looks for it. Only the compliance logo treatment holds it back.

**Typography (8).** The system is solid. The oversized step numerals behind "1" and "2" sit slightly off-centre against the headlines beneath them (see around 0:12). Tighten the centring rule on that absolute container.

**Colour and surface (8).** Ivory and deep blue is the right call. The certificate badge clip is the only visible flaw.

**Motion (7).** Quick and unfussy, but flat. Stripe-grade ease-outs would add the last 10% of polish. Not urgent.

**Hierarchy and layout (8).** Card rhythm is clean on mobile. The footer is the weak point, covered above.

## Reference frame

Mosh would warm the compliance row up with a sentence of plain-English context next to the logos rather than letting them stand alone. Pilot would make the price on the CTA even louder and probably colour-lock the refund line to the button. Stripe would spend a week on the motion curves we're calling a 7. Linear would kill half the footer links before reformatting the rest. The brand is already in that conversation. The fixes above are what closes the gap.
