# The page is honest about money. The hero jitters before it gets the chance to say so.

The bones are right. Clear tiers, plain Australian English about Medicare and fees, trust signals sitting next to the buttons. Then the hero counter jumps from 106% to 100% in the first few seconds and the whole thing feels less certain than it is.

## What's working

- Ivory surfaces and restrained coral accents read calm and clinical, not loud.
- Pricing cards say the number first. No hunting.
- Copy speaks to the two things people actually worry about: Medicare and hidden fees.
- The doctor's signature and the "what we won't do" link are still doing their job.

## What to fix, ranked

### 1. Kill the hero counter jitter

**Impact:** high · **Where:** hero stat block, around 0:04

The refund metric animates from 106% to 100% and the layout shifts with it. First impression, first four seconds, and the page wobbles. Lock a fixed width on the numeric container so the digits change inside a stable frame. If the 106% is an animation flourish, drop it. The number doesn't need a runway.

### 2. Rebuild the "Most Popular" badge

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** Medical Certificate card, around 0:09

The star-icon badge looks like it came from a different site. On a card this clean, it draws the wrong kind of attention. A simple pill in the brand coral, same type stack as the rest of the UI, and it disappears into the design the way it should.

### 3. Break up the mobile wall of text

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** "Understanding telehealth pricing" section, around 0:21

On a 375px screen the explainer reads as one grey slab. Split it into 2-3 sentence paragraphs. Pull the Medicare and refund points into bullets. People scroll-scan pricing pages. Give them edges to land on.

## Full critique by category

**Brand spine (6).** Warm and clear, but not yet as quietly confident as Mosh or Pilot. The page explains. It doesn't quite assert.

**Typography (7).** Jakarta and Source Sans render cleanly on desktop. Mobile is where the long paragraphs flatten the rhythm. Fix the slab and this moves to 8.

**Motion (5).** The hero shift is the whole story. Everything else scrolls fine. Stabilise the counter and the score climbs.

**Hierarchy and layout (6).** Card order is logical and the CTAs sit where the eye lands. The badge is the one weak component on an otherwise tidy grid.

**Signature devices (6).** Signature present, refund link present, but no live wait time near the cards. A small "doctor available in ~7 minutes" indicator beside the primary CTA would do more work than another testimonial.

## Reference frame

Mosh would tighten the hero to one number and one promise, no animated counter, no jitter. Pilot would put the wait time in the sticky bar and let the price card breathe. Stripe would never ship the star-icon badge. The page is closer to that bar than the score suggests. Fix the four-second wobble and the badge, and most of the gap closes on its own.
