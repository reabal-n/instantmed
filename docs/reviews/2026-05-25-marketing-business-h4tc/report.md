# The copy is doing the heavy lifting. The form is undoing it.

The page reads like a GP wrote it, which is rare and worth protecting. But the seven-field contact form at the end of the scroll throws away the goodwill the words just built. Fix the form, add some movement, and this clears the bar.

## What's working

- The voice. Calm, direct, no jargon. Best category on the page.
- The live wait-counter is sitting where it should and earning its keep.
- Ivory background and type hierarchy land the "considered, not clinical" feel.
- The hero gets to the B2B point fast without selling.

## What to fix, ranked

### 1. The contact form has seven fields. It should have two.

**Impact:** high · **Where:** contact section, around the 0:17 mark

An HR manager scoping vendors at 4pm on a Tuesday will not fill seven fields. Ask for work email and company name. Everything else (headcount, role, timeline) can come after they hit submit, as a progressive step or a reply from a real person. The form is the moment trust converts. Right now it leaks.

### 2. The page loads like a PDF.

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** hero and section entries, from 0:05

Nothing fades, nothing settles. A 300ms ease-out on the hero card and section reveals on scroll will do the work. Not animation for its own sake, just enough to signal the product is alive. Linear and Stripe do this in their sleep.

### 3. The benefit cards are a wall of text.

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** features grid, around 0:10

On mobile the descriptions read as one continuous block. Bold the first two or three words of each, or pull a short lead line above the body copy. Give the eye something to land on.

## Full critique by category

**Brand spine (7).** The B2B proposition is clear but the signature line is missing. An adapted sub-head like "Faster for your team than a clinic visit" would tie this page back to the consumer brand without copying it word for word.

**Color and surface (8).** Cards sit slightly flat on the ivory. A cool-tinted shadow instead of neutral grey gives the depth the palette is asking for.

**Motion (5).** Covered above. The lowest score on the page and the easiest to lift.

**Hierarchy and layout (8).** Good rhythm on desktop, tight on mobile inside the benefits grid. Bolded openers fix most of it.

**Conversion friction (6).** Covered above. The single biggest lever on the page.

**Signature devices (7).** The wait-counter is doing its job. The "what we won't certify" link belongs on this page too, near the FAQ. HR teams ask that question before they ask about price.

**Typography (8).** One small thing: the "FOR BUSINESS" eyebrow is set tight. Add a touch of letter-spacing and it reads properly.

## Reference frame

Mosh would keep the voice and rip out five form fields without asking. Pilot would put the price or the per-employee cost above the fold and dare you to keep scrolling. Linear would add the motion this page is missing in an afternoon. Stripe would replace the form with a single email field and a "we'll be in touch within the hour" line, and mean it. The bones here are good. It just needs the confidence to ask for less.
