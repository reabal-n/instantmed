# The homepage earns trust early. Two CTAs share the load when one should lead.

A real doctor, clear pricing, and a hero that says "Faster than your GP" within the first second. The page does the job. A handful of small fixes would take it from polished to quietly decisive.

## What's working

- The hero lands the promise in five words and shows the price without making you ask.
- Ivory backgrounds with soft sky shadows. Reads like a clinic, not a startup.
- Plus Jakarta Sans paired with Source Sans 3 holds up on a 375px screen.
- The signed doctor's name at the footer and the "Last reviewed 16 min ago" tag. Both feel like a real practice.
- Copy that says what it costs and what you get. No corporate filler.

## What to fix, ranked

### 1. The two hero buttons compete

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** hero CTA row (around the 0:01 mark)

"Get started" and "How it works" carry the same visual weight. That splits the click. Make "How it works" a ghost or outline button. Let the primary action win the eye.

### 2. Mobile scroll is longer than it needs to be

**Impact:** medium · **Where:** numbered process section, viewports under 640px (around 0:12)

The large numeric dividers add a lot of vertical air. On a phone that means three extra thumb-swipes to reach the next idea. Tighten the vertical padding on the step cards under 640px.

### 3. The trust badge is too far from the button

**Impact:** low · **Where:** hero (at 0:01)

"Last reviewed 16 min ago" is doing real work, but it's stranded. Move it directly under the primary CTA so the reassurance lands at the moment of decision.

## Full critique by category

**Typography (8).** Type system is sound. One snag: the "Planned" subtext under "Coming Next" (at 0:10) is small and low-contrast on ivory. Bump the weight or size to clear WCAG AA.

**Colour and surface (9).** The warm ivory and sky-shadow combination is the strongest visual signature on the page. Minor: the "Repeat Rx" pill (at 0:05) uses a blue-grey gradient that drifts off-palette. Snap it back to brand values.

**Motion (8).** Scroll-triggered state checklists feel alive without jumping the layout around. When the sidebar nav opens (around 0:24), the page underneath stays sharp and competes for attention. A backdrop blur or dim would settle that.

**Hierarchy and layout (8).** Card groupings are clean and the rhythm reads well on desktop. Mobile is where the spacing tax shows up, see fix #2.

**Conversion friction (8).** Price is visible, the path is short, and the primary button is unmissable. The only drag is the secondary button sitting at equal weight, see fix #1.

**Signature devices (8).** The handwritten signature and live "last reviewed" timestamp are the kind of details that separate a clinic from a landing page. Get them closer to the decision points.

## Reference frame

Mosh would lean harder into the doctor as a person, maybe a face near the hero, not just a signature in the footer. Pilot would collapse the two CTAs into one and trust the page to explain itself on scroll. Linear would cut a third of the vertical space on mobile without blinking. Stripe would already have moved the "Last reviewed" badge under the button six months ago. None of these are large changes. They're the difference between an 8 and a 9.
