# AU Digital-PR / Backlink Plan — free or <$1k AUD (2026-06-04)

**Goal:** earn the off-page AU authority that all three brain reviews keep naming as the binding constraint — so Google indexes + ranks `/medical-certificate`, `/prescriptions`, and the four service lines. White-hat only; a young YMYL domain cannot afford a manual penalty.

**Spend:** essentially $0. Everything below is free + time. Optional paid items capped so the lifetime spend stays well under $1k AUD (see Budget).

**Attribution (privacy-first — operator's name stays OFF, website AND media):** All PR is **company-attributed** — "InstantMed" / "InstantMed's clinical team" / "an AHPRA-registered doctor at InstantMed (not named for privacy)". No personal name, no founder name, no individual doctor name anywhere public. This rules out personal-expert PR and any bio/quote-bank. The trade-off: some journalists prefer a named human, so the **company-attributed DATA ASSET (Pillar 2) becomes the PRIMARY lever** — data stories link to the company, not a person, and need no name. Keep all commentary to TGA-safe lanes (telehealth/access, medical certificates + work, general health navigation); never promote prescription-only meds (ED/hair-loss) — that's TGA advertising territory.

---

## Pillar 1 — Reactive digital PR (company-attributed) — FREE
Earn *editorial* links by answering journalist requests as **InstantMed** (not a named person). Weaker than named-expert PR for some outlets, but works well for telehealth-trend, sick-day, and workplace-evidence stories — especially when paired with a stat from the data asset (Pillar 2). Secondary to Pillar 2 given the no-name constraint.

**Platforms (all free tiers):**
- **HARO / Featured.com** — revived free (3 daily email digests, no login). Sign up as an expert source.
- **Qwoted** — free tier; 70%+ of requests from DR80+ publications. Best quality.
- **JournoFinder Requests** — free aggregator of HARO + Qwoted + #journorequest in one search.
- **#journorequest** on X and Bluesky — journalists post live requests; filter for AU + health/work/lifestyle.
- **SourceBottle** — Australian-founded, free for sources; skews AU media + lifestyle/parenting/HR.

**Cadence:** 2–3 tailored responses/week. Fast, specific, quotable (2–4 sentences + a stat), with a one-line credential and a link to instantmed.com.au. Target AU outlets first (news.com.au, ABC, SMH/Nine, news.com.au verticals, Kidspot, HR/payroll/small-biz trades, uni student media).
**Effort:** ~2–3 hrs/week. **Expected:** 1–3 editorial links/month once warmed up. Compounds.

## Pillar 2 — One original AU data asset (the link magnet) — FREE (internal data)
The highest-leverage single play. InstantMed has proprietary, anonymisable aggregate data (request reasons, timing, day-of-week, by state, telehealth usage) that **no one else has**. Turn it into a recurring, citable report:

- **"The Australian Sick Day Report"** (or "Telehealth Access Index") — e.g., which days/months Australians most request medical certificates, top stated reasons, after-hours demand, state differences, work-from-home vs on-site patterns.
- Built from **fully anonymised, aggregated** data only — no PHI, APP-compliant, legal sign-off before publish. Aggregate counts/percentages, suppress small cells.
- Use the existing infographic pipeline for 2–3 genuinely chart-driven visuals (this is exactly the right use of that moat).
- Host it as an indexable page (add slug to the blog keep-set), then **pitch it** to AU news/business/HR media as an original data story. Refresh quarterly so it stays citable.

Original AU stats are what AU journalists link to. This is the asset that earns DR70+ links no directory ever will. **Effort:** ~1–2 days to build the first edition. **Expected:** the biggest single source of high-authority links over 6–12 months.

## Pillar 3 — Foundational citations (NAP) — FREE, fast, baseline trust
Low individual value (mostly nofollow) but cheap, fast, and a baseline local/entity-trust signal. **Standardise NAP first** (InstantMed Pty Ltd · Level 1/457-459 Elizabeth Street, Surry Hills NSW 2010 · 0450 722 549 · support@instantmed.com.au) and use it identically everywhere.

- **Google Business Profile** — attempt as a service-area / online business using the registered office. *Caveat:* GBP is built for in-person/service-area businesses; a pure-online async service can be hard to verify or get suspended. Worth one careful attempt; don't over-invest. Add **Bing Places** too.
- **Top AU general directories (free):** Yellow Pages AU, True Local, StartLocal, Localsearch, Word of Mouth, Yelp AU, Pink Pages, Cylex, Hotfrog, dLook, AussieWeb. ~10–15 listings, one sitting.
- **Skip** the health-booking directories (HealthEngine, HotDoc, Whitecoat) — they're for appointment-booking clinics; InstantMed's form-first async model doesn't fit and won't benefit.
**Effort:** ~half a day total. **Expected:** entity consistency + a handful of links; do once, then forget.

## Pillar 4 — Relationship / niche-relevant links — FREE, relationship-based
Genuinely relevant AU placements (not spam):
- **Small-business / HR / payroll AU blogs + Fair Work explainer sites** — pitch a genuinely useful expert piece on the *medical-certificate evidence* angle (what employers can/can't require, Fair Work rules). High relevance to `/medical-certificate`.
- **AU university student services + accommodation/coworking blogs** — "medical certificates for students/special consideration" angle.
- **AU startup/founder communities** (the operator's network) — a founder-story or telehealth-build post on a reputable AU startup outlet earns a contextual link.
- **Partnerships:** AU pharmacies, allied-health, or employee-wellbeing providers — reciprocal, relevant mentions.
**Effort:** ongoing outreach, ~10–15 targeted asks per quarter.

## What to AVOID (would hurt a young YMYL domain)
- Paid link packages, "guest post networks" (guestpostlinks.net-type), Fiverr "1,000 backlinks," PBNs, mass auto-directory blasts, link exchanges at scale. These violate Google's link-spam guidelines and risk a manual action that would be catastrophic here.
- Press-release wire distribution (Medianet/AAP ~$100–500) — mostly nofollow syndication, low ROI; skip unless a release accompanies the data report and even then optional.

## 90-day sequence
- **Weeks 1–2:** NAP standardised → GBP/Bing + 10–15 AU directories (Pillar 3). Sign up to HARO/Featured, Qwoted, JournoFinder, #journorequest, SourceBottle. Write Dr Najjar's expert bio + 3–4 commentary lanes + a quote bank. Start pitching (Pillar 1).
- **Weeks 3–8:** Sustained 2–3 PR responses/week. Build edition 1 of the data asset (pull + anonymise + legal sign-off + visuals + page).
- **Weeks 6–10:** Launch the data report; pitch it to 10–15 AU outlets. Begin Pillar 4 outreach (HR/Fair Work + student + founder).
- **Ongoing:** track new referring domains; double down on whatever earns links; refresh the data asset quarterly.

## Budget (lifetime, well under $1k AUD)
- Pillars 1–4: **$0** (time only).
- Optional: Qwoted paid tier (~$100–150/mo) *only if* the free tier proves limiting — start free, upgrade one month if warranted. Optional one-off designer for the report if the infographic pipeline isn't enough (~$100–300). **Realistic actual spend: $0–300.**

## Metrics (check monthly via `pnpm seo:gsc-index-audit` + Google Search Console + free Ahrefs Webmaster Tools)
- New **referring domains** (the headline number) + their DR.
- Indexation status of `/medical-certificate` + `/prescriptions` (Discovered → Indexed is the win condition).
- Branded search volume + homepage impressions trend.
- Per-link: relevance + dofollow/nofollow + DR. Quality over count — 5 relevant DR50+ AU editorial links beat 200 directories.
