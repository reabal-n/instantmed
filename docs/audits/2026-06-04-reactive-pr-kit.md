## Step 2 — Stay in your lanes (TGA/AHPRA-safe)

Only answer queries that fall in these four lanes. Outside them, skip.

1. **Telehealth access & trends** — how online consults work, who they suit, after-hours/rural access, what telehealth can and can't do, safety/triage. *(Avoid: naming or comparing prescription meds.)*
2. **Medical certificates & the workplace** — Fair Work evidence rules, what employers can/can't require, single vs multi-day, carer's leave, the "Monday sickie" myth. **Highest-relevance lane** (maps to /medical-certificate).
3. **Sick-day & seasonal-illness behaviour** — winter demand, cold/flu vs see-a-doctor, when to rest vs escalate. Great for lifestyle/HR outlets; pairs with the data asset.
4. **Navigating online health safely** — spotting legitimate vs dodgy online services, AHPRA registration, what a real clinical assessment looks like.

**Never:** give individual medical advice in a quote; promote/compare prescription-only medicines (ED, hair-loss, weight-loss drugs = TGA advertising breach); claim "accepted by all employers" or court/exam/special-consideration outcomes; name a doctor or your founder; claim doctor count / FRACGP / peer review / team size.

## Step 3 — Response templates (company-attributed, 2–4 sentences, quotable)

Rules that win HARO/Qwoted links: **be fast** (first good answer often wins), **specific** (a number or a concrete rule beats vagueness), **self-contained** (usable verbatim), **link once** at the end. Forward me any live query and I'll tailor one of these.

**A) "Why do so many sick-leave requests land on Mondays / how has telehealth changed sick days?"**
> "We see demand concentrate at the start of the week and outside business hours — people manage a sick day around work and family rather than a clinic's opening times. Telehealth hasn't created more illness; it's shifted when and how people seek documentation, letting someone who's genuinely unwell sort a medical certificate without dragging themselves into a waiting room. — InstantMed, an Australian telehealth service (instantmed.com.au)"

**B) "Can an employer demand a medical certificate for one sick day?"**
> "Under the Fair Work Act, employers can ask for 'reasonable evidence' that leave is genuine — and that can include a single day, if it's a consistent, reasonable policy. A medical certificate or a statutory declaration both qualify. What an employer can't do is demand a diagnosis; a certificate only needs to state you were unfit for work for the period. — InstantMed (instantmed.com.au)"

**C) "Is telehealth safe / when shouldn't you use it?"**
> "Telehealth works well for low-risk, well-defined needs — a sick-leave certificate, a repeat of a stable medication, a straightforward consult. The safety comes from the clinician's judgement: an AHPRA-registered doctor should decline or redirect you to in-person care when something needs an examination or doesn't add up. Red-flag symptoms — chest pain, breathing difficulty, severe or sudden symptoms — are always an in-person or emergency matter, not telehealth. — InstantMed (instantmed.com.au)"

**D) "Can you get a mental health day as sick leave?"**
> "In Australia, mental health is health — personal/carer's leave covers it the same as a physical illness, and a certificate can support a mental-health sick day without disclosing a diagnosis. The certificate states you were unfit for work for the period; the clinical detail stays between you and the doctor. — InstantMed (instantmed.com.au)"

**E) "Winter/cold-and-flu season — when should you see a doctor?"**
> "Most colds and flu are viral and settle with rest, fluids and time — antibiotics don't help and aren't prescribed for them. See a doctor if you're short of breath, symptoms last beyond about 10 days or worsen after improving, you have a high fever you can't control, or you're in a higher-risk group. Telehealth is a sensible first step for documentation or advice when you're unsure. — InstantMed (instantmed.com.au)"

> Each template is pre-checked against the guardrails above (no drug promotion, no SLA, no named clinician, no outcome guarantees). When a real query comes in, send it to me and I'll match + tailor.
