# InstantMed Business Plan

> Canonical business strategy for the current operating phase.
> Read this file before changing service positioning, pricing, growth strategy, landing pages, ads, or roadmap priorities.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-28

---

## 1. Current Strategic Verdict

InstantMed is a specialised, form-first Australian telehealth service. It is not a broad online GP clinic.

The current goal is to release and scale the platform to its realistic solo-doctor revenue ceiling. Acquisition strategy is intentionally out of scope until the business has stable revenue, clean compliance, clear unit economics, and staff capacity.

## 2. Non-Negotiable Business Decisions

| Decision | Rule |
|----------|------|
| **Business model** | One-off transactions only for now. No subscriptions, memberships, bundles, or pharmacy fulfilment until there is staff capacity. |
| **Fulfilment** | eScript token only. No owned pharmacy, delivery, inventory, or dispensing margin in this phase. |
| **Clinic positioning** | Specialised services only: med certs, repeat prescriptions, hair loss, ED, weight loss, women's health. |
| **Moat** | No booking friction: patients start with a secure clinical form and avoid a call unless the doctor decides more information is clinically needed. |
| **Clinical control** | The doctor can call or message any patient at any time. The website must never promise that prescribing requests will never need doctor contact. |
| **Primary growth target** | $1M AUD annual gross revenue from one-off services before expanding into subscriptions or staff-heavy care models. |

## 3. Product Priority

| Rank | Service | Role | Rationale |
|------|---------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Medical certificates | Revenue engine | Lowest operational friction, strongest solo-doctor fit, supports protocolised safe automation for suitable requests. |
| 2 | Repeat prescriptions | Second core product | Strong trust expansion after med cert use, one-off revenue, clearer patient intent than broad consults. |
| 3 | Hair loss | Specialist upsell | Good future subscription potential, currently one-off only, lower acute risk than ED or weight loss. |
| 4 | ED | Specialist upsell | High intent and good AOV, but higher contraindication, privacy, and advertising risk. |
| 5 | Women's health | Narrow specialist line | Keep scope tight and protocol-led. Avoid broad "women's health clinic" positioning. |
| 6 | Weight loss | Manual/high-risk line | Highest monitoring and advertising risk. Keep manual-only until staffing and follow-up capacity exist. |

General consults are not a brand pillar. They are a fallback pathway for cases that do not fit the specialised service lines.

## 4. Positioning

### Platform Positioning

InstantMed gives Australians a faster way to request common medical services without booking an appointment or sitting in a waiting room.

### Approved Platform Wedge

Use:

> No appointment. No waiting room. Start with a secure clinical form.

### Approved Prescribing/Specialty Wedge

Use:

> Complete a secure clinical form. A doctor contacts you only if more information is clinically needed.

### Approved Med Cert Wedge

Use only on med-cert surfaces:

> No video. No call. No appointment.

### Do Not Use

- "No doctor"
- "Guaranteed prescription"
- "Guaranteed treatment"
- "No call needed" on prescription, ED, hair loss, women's health, or weight loss pages
- "Doctor approves in 2 hours or your money back"
- "Accepted by all employers"
- "98% accepted"
- "Special consideration" or "deferred exam" as supported use cases

## 5. Revenue Strategy

The near-term business should optimise for:

1. High-converting med-cert traffic.
2. Repeat-script cross-sell after trust is established.
3. Hair-loss and ED specialist pages for higher AOV.
4. Manual-only weight loss until staff and follow-up systems exist.
5. Lower support tickets per order, not just higher volume.

The fastest way to lose money is to scale paid traffic into unclear eligibility, refund-heavy flows, unsupported claims, or manual support debt.

## 6. Solo-Doctor Operating Model

The solo-doctor phase works only if the platform reduces doctor minutes per order without weakening clinical accountability.

| Service | Solo phase operating rule |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Med certs | Protocolised automation for suitable low-risk requests, with doctor batch review and QA sampling. |
| Repeat prescriptions | Doctor-reviewed one-off eScript request. Call/message if unclear, new, unstable, high-risk, or incomplete. |
| Hair loss | One-off form-first doctor assessment. No subscription, no outcome guarantee, no drug-name marketing. |
| ED | One-off form-first doctor assessment. Strong contraindication screening. Doctor contact if any cardiac, medication, identity, or history concern. |
| Women's health | Narrowly scoped services only. Anything complex routes to GP/in-person care. |
| Weight loss | Manual review only. No automated approval. No ongoing monitoring promise in this phase. |

## 7. Current Moat

The moat is not "no doctor." That is a bad claim and a regulatory liability.

The moat is:

- no booked appointment
- no waiting room
- form-first clinical intake
- fast doctor review
- clear pricing
- refund if the doctor cannot help
- eScript token to any Australian pharmacy
- med cert automation for safe administrative requests

## 8. 10/10 Business Definition

InstantMed reaches 10/10 for this phase when:

- compliance copy is clean across marketing, ads, metadata, schema, and emails
- no prescription drug page is used as a paid landing page
- no service promises no doctor contact for prescribing requests
- the med-cert automation protocol is logged, auditable, and doctor-owned
- the business dashboard shows conversion, refund, chargeback, doctor minutes, support load, AOV, and repeat usage by service
- solo-doctor capacity is protected by triage, kill switches, support templates, and clear decline/refund flows
- $1M/year is mathematically supported by actual service mix and order volume, not traffic assumptions

## 9. Expansion Gates

Do not add subscriptions, pharmacy fulfilment, staff-heavy follow-up, or broader GP services until all of these are true:

- monthly gross revenue is consistently above $60k
- refund rate is below 8-10%
- chargebacks are below 0.5%
- support tickets stay below 5 per 100 orders
- med-cert median turnaround is below 30 minutes
- doctor queue P95 is below 2 hours during operating hours
- clinical QA is current
- advertising and SEO pages pass compliance checks
